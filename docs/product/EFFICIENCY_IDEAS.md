# Arc efficiency and workflow ideas

## Best next improvements

1. **Duplicate a question row** — speeds up entry for papers with many subparts that share a topic and adjacent PDF pages.
2. **CSV question-map import/export** — teachers or teammates can map a paper in a spreadsheet and upload all question parts at once.
3. **Draft autosave** — preserves partially mapped papers before they are ready for the library.
4. **Page thumbnail picker** — lets a student choose question and solution pages visually instead of typing page numbers.
5. **Validation dashboard** — flags missing pages, duplicate labels, unusual mark totals and questions without solutions before publishing.
6. **Reusable topic presets** — offers the correct NESA topic list for the selected course and remembers recently used topics.
7. **Copy from a previous paper** — reuses time, course, sections and common question structures for another school paper.
8. **Bulk editing** — applies one topic, page offset or section to several selected question parts.
9. **Keyboard-first entry** — Enter creates the next part, shortcuts duplicate/remove rows, and focus moves in a predictable order.
10. **Private sharing link** — lets a student share a completed paper map with a teammate after explicit consent and server-side storage are available.

## Suggested order

Build draft autosave, duplicate-row controls and NESA topic presets first. They reduce repetitive entry without requiring a backend. CSV import/export and private sharing are more powerful, but need stronger validation and a secure account-based storage service.
